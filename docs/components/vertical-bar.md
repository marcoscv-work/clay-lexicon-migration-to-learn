# Vertical Bar

Use a vertical bar as a flexible container that organizes items vertically on
the left or right edge of the screen, with a panel that expands and collapses.
It is common in direct manipulation interfaces (like a page editor: layout,
fragments, hierarchy) and in document previews with metadata panels.

## Variants and anatomy

The bar has three parts: **tab items**, optional **dividers** that group them,
and the **panel** each tab opens. Items respond with default, hover, disabled,
active, and focus states.

Items stack in three areas (top, center, bottom) in any combination. The bar
comes in light and dark variations and accepts custom styling. The panel can
open by pushing the content aside (push/pull) or by hovering over it, and the
bar can sit on either edge, opening left or right.

Try the [Vertical Bar story](https://storybook.clayui.com/?path=/story/design-system-components-verticalbar--default)
and the [dynamic content story](https://storybook.clayui.com/?path=/story/design-system-components-verticalbar--dynamic-content).

## Usage guidelines

- Give every icon-only item a tooltip of one or two words naming its action.
- Group related items with dividers instead of mixing unrelated tools.
- Keep one item active at a time; clicking the active item toggles its panel.

## Implementation

The component ships in `@clayui/core`:

```shell
npm install @clayui/css @clayui/core
```

Compose the bar with its content and panels:

```jsx
import {VerticalBar} from '@clayui/core';
import Icon from '@clayui/icon';

export default function Example() {
	return (
		<VerticalBar position="right">
			<VerticalBar.Content displayType="light">
				<VerticalBar.Item>
					<button aria-label="Show details" className="btn btn-unstyled">
						<Icon symbol="info-circle" />
					</button>
				</VerticalBar.Item>
			</VerticalBar.Content>
			<VerticalBar.Panel>
				<div className="p-3">Details panel content.</div>
			</VerticalBar.Panel>
		</VerticalBar>
	);
}
```

## Accessibility

- Once focus enters the bar, `Tab` leaves it and the up and down arrows move
  between tab items, wrapping at the ends.
- Icon-only items carry accessible names in addition to their tooltips.
- The open panel is a labeled region tied to its tab item.
